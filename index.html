About Me

Hello, my name is Ting-Wei Chen. I am a graduate student in Chemical Engineering at Oregon State University. My academic background includes transport phenomena, reaction engineering, process modeling, materials characterization, and technical communication.

Through coursework and research projects, I have gained experience in ceramic processing, high-temperature porous materials, COMSOL-based modeling, experimental design, and data interpretation. My professional interests include process engineering, semiconductor manufacturing, materials research, and advanced manufacturing systems.

Solid Catalyzed Reaction Processes

Developed a mathematical and computational model for transport and reaction behavior in a wall-coated catalytic microreactor. The project included flow assumptions, governing equations, velocity profile derivation, mass transfer analysis, reaction kinetics, and COMSOL concentration distribution analysis.

Focus: Transport modeling, catalytic surface reactions, COMSOL simulation.

Darcy Flow in Porous Media

Presented the fundamentals of Darcy flow and explained how pressure gradient, permeability, and viscosity control fluid movement through porous materials. The project connected fluid mechanics concepts to filtration, groundwater movement, and porous engineering systems.

Focus: Porous media flow, permeability, engineering communication.
