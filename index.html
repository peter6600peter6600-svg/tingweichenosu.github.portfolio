About Me

Hello, my name is Ting-Wei Chen. I am a graduate student in Chemical Engineering at Oregon State University. My academic background includes transport phenomena, reaction engineering, process modeling, materials characterization, and technical communication.

Through research and coursework, I have gained experience in ceramic processing, high-temperature porous materials, COMSOL-based simulation, Julia programming, experimental design, and data interpretation. My professional interests include process engineering, semiconductor manufacturing, materials research, and advanced manufacturing systems.

CHE 540 – Microreactor Modeling with COMSOL

Developed a mathematical and computational model for transport and reaction behavior in a wall-coated catalytic microreactor. The project included governing equations, velocity profile derivation, mass transfer analysis, reaction kinetics, and COMSOL concentration distribution analysis.

Focus: Transport modeling, catalytic surface reactions, COMSOL simulation.

CHE 525 – Lidocaine Dynamics Using Julia

Developed a two-compartment pharmacokinetic model using Julia to simulate lidocaine movement between bloodstream and tissue compartments. The project applied ordinary differential equations, eigenvalue analysis, matrix exponential methods, and data visualization.

Focus: Julia programming, ODE systems, linear algebra, and pharmacokinetic modeling.
